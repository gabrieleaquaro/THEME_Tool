EVALUATION OF SAFEGUARD BARRIERS

Based on the observations of the work environment conducted at the site, select the answer that best reflects the observed behaviors:

NON-TECHNICAL SKILLS

Is the worker, with respect to safety issues, capable of:
facilitate decision-making in the group?

prioritise when decisions need to be made?

use the resources available (for example, help from colleagues, superiors...) to protect themselves from situations of potential stress?

recognise the states and causes of physical fatigue?

adopt strategies to deal with mental fatigue?

have an adequate view of the various risks present in the work environment?

effectively transmit information to colleagues and superiors?

understand information received from colleagues and superiors?

Total: -

TECHNICAL SKILLS

Does the worker:
possess the technical knowledge necessary to work in a safe manner?

have the ability to perform tasks in a safe manner?

have the ability to implement the emergency procedures?

have the ability to perform the tasks in a safe manner even in the face of unforeseen events?

Total: -

SAFETY MOTIVATION

does the worker comply with safety rules and procedures:
to obtain approval from others?

to avoid being criticised?

out of fear of losing his/her job?

for the pleasure of working safely?

because they represent important values for him/her?

Total: -

BELONGING

Within the working groups, do workers:
volunteer for tasks associated with safety processes?

help colleagues by teaching them the safety procedures?

help each other by sharing the responsibilities associated with safety?

encourage each other to become more involved in safety issues?

intervene actively in order to prevent potential breaches of safety rules and procedures?

communicate to colleagues that they follow safety rules and procedures?

communicate to newcomers that safety breaches are not tolerated?

keep abreast of changes in safety rules and procedures?

Total: -

LEADERSHIP HSE

Do the supervisors:
establish clear responsibilities for HSE?

set clear HSE objectives?

encourage workers to suggest HSE improvements?

underline the importance of wearing PPE?

ensure that all HSE standards are complied with, not only the most important ones?

intervene immediately in the event of a breach of HSE rules/procedures?

act as examples of compliance with HSE standards?

require attention to health and safety at all times (including at the end of the work shift)?

Total: -

SAFETY CULTURE

In general, in the organization is it perceives that:
the management puts strong emphasis on work-related HSE topics?

the management intervenes before incidents occur?

the management considers HSE as important as production?

the management intervenes when workers do not comply with the HSE rules?

workers and management work together to ensure the best possible HSE conditions?

HSE standards and procedures are useful?

HSE standards and procedures are easy to apply?

the work environment is appropriate to the HSE standards?

the work tools are adequate for the HSE standards?

PPE are appropriate to the safety standards?
